# UI/UX Design

## Library Structure  
- The library has three sections: Currently Reading, Read, and Want to Read.  
- These options appear in the navigation bar when users open the Books tab.  
- Inside Currently Reading, books are grouped by genre (e.g., Fiction, Classic, Romance, or Fantasy).  
- The search bar allows users to find books quickly, and next to each result, there are two options:  
  - Add to Want to Read  
  - Add to Read  
- This setup helps users:  
  - Track what they’re reading right now.  
  - Review completed books with notes and ratings.  
  - Plan future reads and stay organized.  

---

## Navigation Flow  
- BookBuddy uses a simple and consistent navigation bar at the top or side for easy access.  
- Main pages include:  
  - Home: Shows monthly reading progress, quick stats, and shortcuts.  
  - Library: Displays all books organized into Currently Reading, Read, and Want to Read tabs.  
  - Monthly Tracker: Displays current month’s goals and the list of books to be finished.  
  - Settings: Includes About BookBuddy,and Profile section.  
  - Profile: Displays and allows editing of user information such as name, address, and phone number.  
- The navigation stays consistent across all screens to make the app simple and user-friendly.  

---

## Interaction Design  
- Book cards include quick-action buttons like Add, Edit, Delete, and Mark as Read.  
- A search bar connected to the Open Library API lets users find books instantly and add them to Read or Want to Read.  
- The app gives visual feedback using:  
  - Progress bars for reading completion  
  - Star ratings  
  - Check marks for completed books  
- Accessibility is supported with:  
  - Large, easy-to-tap buttons  
  - Clear text labels and icons  
  - Keyboard navigation for all users  

---

## Book Details View  
- For every book in the library, users can open a Book Details page.  
- This page displays:  
  - The book cover, title, and author  
  - The genre and reading status (e.g., Currently Reading, Read, or Want to Read)  
  - The user’s personal note, where they can write thoughts or reflections about the book  
  - Star ratings to show how much they liked it  
  - A section for reviews, where users can write or edit their feedback about the book  
- Users can edit or delete their notes and ratings at any time.  
- This page helps readers keep a record of their experience with each book.  

---

## Tracker Functionality  
- The Monthly Tracker shows the list of books planned for the current month.  
- Each book displays its title, cover, and reading status with options:  
  - Mark as Read  
  - Mark as Not Read  
- Users can track their completion rate and know how many books are left for the month.  
- The goal is to finish all listed books by the end of the month.  
- Clicking on a book in the tracker opens its details page, where users can view notes, ratings, and reviews.  

---

## Settings Section  
- The Settings page helps users personalize their experience.  
- It includes three sections:  
  - About BookBuddy: Shows a short description of the app and its purpose.  
  - Dark Mode: Provides a toggle switch for switching between light and dark themes.  
  - Profile: Opens the user’s personal information page.  

---

## Profile Section  
- The Profile page displays the details entered during sign-up, such as:  
  - Name  
  - Email  
  - Address  
  - Phone number  
- Users can click Edit Profile to update any information.  
- All changes are saved automatically and shown across the app.  

---

## Key Interactions  
- Sign Up: Users create an account by entering their personal details.  
- Login: Users log in to access their saved books and progress.  
- Home: Displays reading stats and shortcuts to the Library, Tracker, and Settings.  
- Books: Lets users browse all books, open book details, change status, edit or delete entries, and view by genre.  
- Search: Allows users to find books and add them directly to Read or Want to Read.  
- Book Details: Shows the book’s cover, author, notes, ratings, and reviews.  
- Tracker: Displays the month’s reading list and lets users update progress.  
- Settings: Provides options for Dark Mode, app info, and accessing the Profile page.  
- Profile: Displays and allows editing of user details.  

---

## Example User Flow  
- The user opens BookBuddy and logs in.  
- On the Home page, they see reading progress and quick links.  
- The user taps Books to view Currently Reading, Read, or Want to Read.  
- In Currently Reading, books are organized by genre.  
- The user searches for a new book and adds it to Want to Read.  
- They open a book’s details page to write a note, give a rating, and add a review.  
- In the Tracker, they check their monthly goal and mark completed books as Read.  
- The user visits Settings to turn on Dark Mode or open their Profile.  
- In Profile, they update their address and phone number.  

---

## Application in BookBuddy  
- The design begins with wireframes for all main screens: Sign Up, Login, Home, Books, Book Details, Monthly Tracker, Settings, and Profile.  
- After the layout is finalized, each screen becomes a mockup with BookBuddy’s colors, icons, and text styles.  
- This process helps the team:  
  - Test how the app works early in development.  
  - Keep a clean and consistent design.  
  - Present realistic screens during sprint reviews and documentation.
